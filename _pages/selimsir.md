Dr. Selim Jahan is an eminent development economist, widely recognized for his distinguished contributions during his tenure as Former Director of the Human Development Report Office and the Poverty Division at the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), New York, USA. It has been my distinct honor to work under his guidance as a Research Associate for the “AI Unveils Tomorrow Project” at the UNDP Regional Office in Bangkok.

In this role, I undertook several key responsibilities. I produced comprehensive political-economic country analyses, tracing historical development trajectories from independence to the present across six countries: Thailand, Indonesia, Viet Nam, Mongolia, Sri Lanka, and Fiji. I also assessed current economic, societal, environmental, and geopolitical risks for each country, synthesizing insights from diverse sources into structured country briefs for a UNDP regional flagship report. Furthermore, I documented national development frameworks—covering policy objectives, strategic priorities, reported achievements, and implementation challenges—to support comparative analysis across the Asia-Pacific region.

Currently, I am working with him in a major book project that examines the evolution of business and trade in Bangladesh from 1947 to the present. This research spans three significant historical periods—1947–1971, 1972–1990, and 1990–present—and analyzes the development, trends, regional distribution, and socio-economic impacts of key sectors, including tea, jute, textiles, ready-made garments, leather, pharmaceuticals, frozen foods, shipbreaking and heavy industries, IT and software, electronics and light engineering, and agro-processing.

**Dr. Jahan has been familiar with my academic and research work since November 2025.**
